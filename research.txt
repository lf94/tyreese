This document is a compilation of research that others have carried out on readability. t uses this research to bring great readability to its users.

A survey with a rather small sample set that describes color preference for both long and short texts; black and white wins.
http://www.laurenscharff.com/research/survreslts.html

A paper looking at fonts and colors.
http://www.laurenscharff.com/research/AHNCUR.html

More study on why "bright on dark" colorschemes are bad for you.
http://graphicdesign.stackexchange.com/a/15152

CPL is Characters Per Line. Why does this matter? Because your eyes lose focus on lines that are too long. The optimal range is 50-75 cpl. t used to use the average, 66 cpl, but has given that up because resizing the editor window is necessary for flexibility.
http://baymard.com/blog/line-length-readability
